## 1. The question this appendix decides

FRC 100.002 v2.5 Â§5.2 defines the **structure functional**

$$
\Sigma(t)=S_{\rm vM}\!\big(C(t)\big)-S\!\big(x(t)\big)\ge 0, \tag{1}
$$

with $C=|\langle e^{ix}\rangle|$ the mean resultant length (566.030, 840.101), $S$ the differential entropy of the phase marginal, and $S_{\rm vM}(C)$ the exact von Mises maximum-entropy curve of 566.030. The inequality is a max-entropy theorem, so $\Sigma$ measures organization the marginal carries beyond its first-moment coherence. The 100.002 pilot showed $\Sigma(\infty)>0$ persists in the mixed regime and hypothesized (Â§5.4) it is anchored on the KAM hierarchy but deferred the direct comparison. This appendix asks two separable questions: does the *value* of $\Sigma(\infty)$ follow without tuning from the measured accessible geometry (magnitude); and is that value set by the *arrangement* of the regular region or merely its area (arrangement)?

## 2. Two operationally independent instruments

The instruments are **operationally independent in the load-bearing sense: the geometric predictor (Instrument B) never consumes the measured $\Sigma$ (Instrument A).** They share the map, the drive $K$, the physical seed region, and the functional form (1); Instrument B contains no random numbers and reads no ensemble output.

### 2.1 Instrument A â€” Î£(âˆž), seed-averaged

Ensemble $x\sim\mathrm{vonMises}(0,8)$, $p\sim\mathcal N(0,0.05)$, $N=4\times10^4$, $T=300$, under the Standard Map

$$
p_{n+1}=p_n+K_{\rm drive}\sin x_n,\qquad x_{n+1}=x_n+p_{n+1}\!\!\pmod{2\pi}. \tag{2}
$$

$\Sigma(\infty)$ is the last-50-step mean of $S_{\rm vM}(C)-S-(M-1)/2N$, $M=72$ bins, with $(M-1)/2N=8.9\times10^{-4}$ nats the analytic Millerâ€“Madow bias (not fitted). Each $K$ is run for 12 independent seeds; we report $\Sigma_{\rm meas}\pm\mathrm{SE}$.

**Cylinder vs torus.** Equation (2) reduces only $x$ modulo $2\pi$; Instrument A evolves on the cylinder and lets $p$ diffuse, as required to measure the true marginal. The Standard Map is $2\pi$-periodic in $p$, so its invariant-set structure is identical on the $2\pi$-torus; Instrument B reduces $p\bmod2\pi$ only to build the geometric projection. Same dynamics, different projection.

### 2.2 Instrument B â€” accessible phase-space geometry (zero fitted parameters)

On a torus grid $(x,p)\in[0,2\pi)^2$, the finite-time Lyapunov exponent from the renormalized tangent map of (2),

$$
\delta p'=\delta p+K_{\rm drive}\cos x\,\delta x,\qquad \delta x'=\delta x+\delta p', \tag{3}
$$

over $T_{\rm ly}$ steps, classifies a cell as **regular** if $\lambda<\lambda_{\rm thr}$; $f_{\rm island}=1-\langle\mathbb 1[\lambda\ge\lambda_{\rm thr}]\rangle$. A periodic flood-fill (union-find across torus boundaries) isolates the chaotic component(s) touching the ensemble seed region. **The seed region uses wrapped toroidal distance:** a grid point $p$ (represented in $[0,2\pi)$) is in the seed if $d_{\mathbb T}(p,0)=\min(p,2\pi-p)<0.2$, and likewise $d_{\mathbb T}(x,0)<0.5$ â€” implemented as `(g<0.2)|(g>2Ï€âˆ’0.2)`, so the region straddling $p=0$ (where $p\sim\mathcal N(0,0.05)$ actually lives) is captured. Verified in code (`accessible_component`). Assuming a uniform distribution on the accessible component, its $x$-marginal $\rho(x)$ gives

$$
C_{\rm pred}=\Big|\sum_x \rho(x)e^{ix}\Big|,\quad
S_{\rm pred}=-\!\sum_x\rho\ln\rho+\ln\tfrac{2\pi}{G},\quad
\Sigma_{\rm pred}=S_{\rm vM}(C_{\rm pred})-S_{\rm pred}. \tag{4}
$$

**Zero fitted parameters, not choice-free.** No fitted amplitude enters (4). There are declared operational choices â€” $\lambda_{\rm thr}$, $T_{\rm ly}$, flood-fill connectivity, seed region, grid $G$, and the uniform-component assumption â€” each fixed before scoring; convergence in the first five is in Â§4. **Two assumptions are probed by two distinct tests, which must not be conflated:** the *uniform-fill* assumption (that the late ensemble fills its accessible component uniformly) is tested by the sub-$K_c$ dynamical failure (Â§6); whether *spatial arrangement* matters given the excluded area is tested by the shuffle null (Â§5). They are different questions.

## 5. Statistical geometry null: arrangement vs area

To separate *arrangement* from *area*, we compare the real $\Sigma_{\rm pred}$ against an **area-preserving shuffle null**: the same *number* of excluded cells placed uniformly at random, destroying spatial arrangement while preserving area. Because only column totals enter the $x$-marginal, uniform fixed-count placement induces a multivariate hypergeometric distribution over columns (each with capacity $G$). We draw **1000 independent shuffles per $K$** ($G=320$) and report mean, standard deviation, 95% interval, and the corrected empirical one-sided tail probability $(b+1)/(1000+1)$, where $b$ is the number of shuffles reaching the real value:

| $K$ | real $\Sigma_{\rm pred}$ | shuffle mean $\pm$ sd | shuffle 95% | $p_{\rm emp}$ | $z$ |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.9716 ($K_c$) | 0.01364 | $0.003773\pm0.000294$ | $[0.003219,0.004355]$ | **$1/1001$** | 33.6 |
| 1.4 | 0.00791 | $0.000916\pm0.000076$ | $[0.000778,0.001068]$ | **$1/1001$** | 92.3 |
| 2.0 | 0.00893 | $0.000523\pm0.000042$ | $[0.000444,0.000607]$ | **$1/1001$** | 198.4 |

The full-torus null (no exclusion) gives $\Sigma_{\rm pred}=0$ trivially.

**Reading.** Across $K=K_c,1.4,2.0$, none of the 1000 shuffled masks reaches the real prediction. With the finite-sample correction, $p_{\rm emp}=1/1001$ at each point; the real values sit $33.6\sigma$, $92.3\sigma$, and $198.4\sigma$ above their null distributions. The **spatial arrangement of the excluded regular region â€” its concentration into coherent blocks that shift the phase marginal off uniform â€” carries the predicted signal, not merely the excluded area.** This is the precise, scoped content of "the shadow of the islands" throughout the estimator-clean window. The earlier $K_c$ area-effect reading resulted from an incorrect repeated-null sampler and is superseded by this independently regenerated fixed-area test.

## 6. Kept negatives, at full volume

**Below $K_c$.** At $K=0.6$ the magnitude prediction fails by $2\times$ ($0.068$ vs $0.033$), not a finite-time transient: extending $T$ to $1000$ and $2000$ moves $\Sigma_{\rm meas}$ only to $0.0308$/$0.0304$. Below $K_c$ the accessible chaotic layer is thin and sticky (cantori, slow transport), so the **uniform-fill assumption** of (4) is wrong. (This is a distinct failure from the $K_c$ arrangement-null result of Â§5: here the *magnitude* is mispredicted because the ensemble does not fill uniformly; there the magnitude is right but is due to area, not arrangement.)

**Above $K\approx2$ (estimator floor).** The histogram-bias correction is 35% of signal at $K=2.5$ and exceeds it (138%) at $K=5.0$; these points are correction-dominated and their low relative errors are not informative. **Usable window: $K_c\le K\lesssim2$.**

Both are retained as `results-negative-kept`.

## 7. What this establishes and does not

**Establishes.** For $K_c\le K\lesssim2$, the mixed-regime $\Sigma(\infty)$ of 100.002 is quantitatively anchored on directly measured invariant-set geometry: a zero-fitted-parameter predictor reproduces its seed-averaged magnitude to $\sim10\%$, beating a fitted one-parameter proxy $2\times$; and a 1000-shuffle fixed-area null establishes that the *arrangement* of the excluded regular region, not its area alone, carries the predicted signal ($33.6$â€“$198.4\sigma$). This converts 100.002 Â§5.4's "consistent with" into a measured, scoped, statistically null-controlled correspondence.

**Does not establish.** (i) Not Gate 4: ranking is matched by raw entropy; discrimination rests on the zero-fitted-parameter magnitude and the arrangement null. (ii) Island *area/arrangement* at fixed $K$, not KAM *survival*. (iii) The FTLE mask marks the **excluded regular region**, which need not be exclusively KAM islands; hence the descriptive title, "shadow of the islands" as physical interpretation for the regime where arrangement dominates. (iv) No quantum gate or cross-mechanism collapse.
