# FRC 826.829 — Mathematical Foundations: Status-Labeled Definitions, Propositions, Conjectures, and Open Problems

## 1. Intent

This paper states the mathematical spine of Fractal Resonance Coherence. It is not a proof that the physical conjectures are true. It is a status-labeled map of the program.

Every claim is assigned one of four statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Definition | a convention or mathematical object introduced by the framework |
| Proposition | a standard computation, symmetry argument, or directly checked identity |
| Conjecture | a physical claim not yet proven |
| Open problem | a precise task left for proof, disproof, or refinement |

The discipline matters. A foundations paper should not hide conjectures inside theorem language. The aim is that a mathematically trained reader can see exactly where to push.

## 2. Coherence and the Lambda Field

For phases $\phi_1,\ldots,\phi_N$ on the circle, a basic coherence parameter is the Kuramoto order-parameter magnitude

$$
C=\left|{1\over N}\sum_{j=1}^{N} e^{i\phi_j}\right|, \qquad C\in(0,1].
$$

The scalar coherence field is

$$
\Lambda=\Lambda_0\ln C,\qquad \Lambda_0>0,
$$

so $\Lambda\le0$ when $C\le1$: full coherence is $\Lambda=0$, the incoherent limit is $\Lambda\to-\infty$. The constant $k^*$ is a **units bridge** — it makes entropy and $\ln C$ additively comparable. It is not a fitted coupling.

## 3. The Open Entropy–Coherence Ledger

The naive expression $dS+k^*d\ln C=d(S+k^*\ln C)$ is exact if $S$ and $C$ are state functions; treating that exact differential as a physical residual would be circular. The FRC ledger is **directional**, not a closed identity. The secure content is the second-law inequality

$$
\sigma_{ST}\equiv dS_{\text{total}}=d_iS\ge0,
$$

and the **combined ledger current** $\sigma_{566}=\sigma_{ST}+k^*d\ln C$ is not sign-definite in general — its sign is directional: $\sigma_{566}\ge\sigma_{ST}\ge0$ when coherence is gained, and $\sigma_{566}\to k^*d\ln C<0$ when coherence is destroyed quasi-statically. The residual is a **measured** current, not an identity: writing $\lambda=-d_eS$ (so $d_iS+k^*d\ln C=\lambda$) forces the conserved closed form $dS+k^*d\ln C=0$, which the open-system reading and direct measurement both reject; it is admissible only as the reversible boundary case. The stronger claim — that the combined current obeys a law of nature with a single units bridge $k^*$ — is a **conjecture**, not a theorem, and its erasure-side (scale-free floor) version was instrumented and **falsified** (100.005 v2.1), leaving the directional/structural conjecture as what survives.

## 4. Effective Dynamics

At the effective level, the Lambda field is modeled by a relaxation-diffusion-advection closure:

$$
\tau_\Lambda(\partial_t\Lambda+u\cdot\nabla\Lambda)
=D_\Lambda\nabla^2\Lambda-\Lambda+\Lambda_{\rm eq}[\rho,\psi,E]+\xi_\Lambda.
$$

This places the effective dynamics in the reaction-diffusion / time-dependent Landau–Ginzburg family. A proposed fundamental scalar Lagrangian

$$
{\cal L}_\Lambda={1\over2}(\partial_\mu\Lambda)(\partial^\mu\Lambda)-V(\Lambda),
\qquad
V(\Lambda)={1\over2}m_\Lambda^2\Lambda^2+{\lambda_4\over4!}\Lambda^4
$$

carries a known tension: the field theory ranges over real $\Lambda$, while the coherence definition requires $\Lambda\le0$ (Section 8).

## 5. The Four Propositions

**Proposition 1 — Two-pole interior band.** Let $F(C)$ be a bounded functional measuring integration that preserves differentiation, $C\in(0,1)$, with both endpoints vanishing: $F(0^+)=0$, $F(1^-)=0$. Then $F$ attains an interior maximum on $(0,1)$. The established statement is the existence of an interior band, not a universal numerical coordinate for it — in particular, no golden-ratio value is asserted.

**Proposition 2 — Forced cubic.** For a coherence expansion $C(m)=1+c_1m+c_2m^2+c_3m^3+O(m^4)$ and reciprocity entropy term $U(m)=-k^*\ln C(m)$, the cubic coefficient is

$$
a_3={k^*\over3}(-c_1^3+3c_1c_2-3c_3).
$$

Unless the odd part of $\ln C$ vanishes through third order, an odd cubic term is forced. The deterministic check with $k^*=1$, $c_1=0.3$, $c_2=0.2$, $c_3=-0.1$ gives $a_3=0.151$. Physical consequences (first-order or tricritical behavior) must still be established per platform.

**Proposition 3 — Critical slowing.** Linearizing the effective dynamics around a fixed point $\Lambda_*$, if $\mu_1$ is the leading eigenvalue, then near a coherence transition

$$
\mu_1\to0^-,
\qquad
\tau_{\rm relax}\sim {1\over |\mu_1|}\to\infty.
$$

This is standard critical slowing; the FRC wager is that coherence transitions in selected systems show measurable slowing in the Lambda channel before collapse or state change.

**Proposition 4 — Von Mises exchange-rate identity.** For the von Mises family $p(\phi)=e^{\kappa\cos(\phi-\mu)}/(2\pi I_0(\kappa))$ with coherence $r=C=I_1(\kappa)/I_0(\kappa)$ and entropy $S(\kappa)=\ln(2\pi I_0(\kappa))-\kappa r$,

$$
{dS\over d\ln C}=-\kappa r,
$$

so the ledger residual along the family is $\lambda=(k^*-\kappa r)\,d\ln C$. For $k^*=1$, closure occurs at exactly one point:

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---:|
| $\kappa$ | 1.608279 |
| $C=r$ | 0.621782 |
| $\kappa r$ | 1.000000 |

This is the analytic form of the peak used in [[FRC-566-030]]. Closure is a special point, not a generic curve — supporting the open-system reading.

## 6. Negative Results: Lambda Is Not Canonical in Two Named Senses

The von Mises family is an exponential family: natural coordinate $\kappa$, expectation coordinate $r=C$, Fisher potential $\ln I_0(\kappa)$. Two candidate claims **fail** on this family:

| Candidate claim | Result |
|---|---|
| $\ln C$ is a dual-affine coordinate | **negative**; $r=C$ is the mixture-affine coordinate |
| $\ln C$ is the Fisher potential | **negative**; $\ln I_0(\kappa)$ is the potential whose Hessian gives the Fisher metric |

This is an important loss. It removes a tempting route by which $\Lambda=\Lambda_0\ln C$ could be called canonical. The surviving information-geometric question is narrower: whether the open production form $d_iS+k^*d\ln C$ has a connection, curvature, or holonomy interpretation on a suitable statistical bundle. These losses narrow the program rather than weaken it, and they are recorded at the same volume as the confirmations.

## 7. Conjectures

| Conjecture | Current status |
|---|---|
| The open ledger holds as a law of nature with one units bridge $k^*$ | useful and testable, but not proven; the von Mises rate $\kappa r$ is state-dependent and therefore a headwind to the strongest reading |
| Collapse dynamics recovers the Born rule as an attractor | supported only in equilibrium-limit arguments; a rigorous H-theorem remains open |
| Specific constants sit at coherence-ease thresholds | high risk; only defensible where the threshold is independently defined |

Their failure would not erase the definitions or the propositions.

## 8. Half-Line Obstruction

The coherence definition requires $\Lambda\le0$, but a fluctuating real scalar field does not automatically respect that half-line. The deterministic appendix computes the positive-tail probability for an unconstrained Gaussian fluctuation around a negative mean:

| Mean Lambda | Fluctuation scale | Fraction with $\Lambda>0$ |
|---:|---:|---:|
| -0.99 | 0.22 | 0.000003 |
| -0.51 | 0.42 | 0.112319 |
| -0.57 | 0.69 | 0.204377 |
| -0.34 | 0.57 | 0.275424 |

When the fluctuation scale becomes comparable to the distance from the boundary, the field spends order-ten-percent time outside the physical region. Two paths exist: derive the half-line as an effective coarse-grained constraint, or reparametrize the fundamental field so $\Lambda<0$ intrinsically — at the cost of the clean polynomial scalar theory.

## 9. The Six Open Problems

| Open problem | Task |
|---|---|
| UCC well-posedness | prove existence, uniqueness, regularity, and positivity for the effective Lambda PDE |
| Born H-theorem | prove or disprove a Lyapunov functional whose equilibrium is the Born distribution |
| Connection or holonomy form | determine whether the open ledger is a connection or curvature object |
| Fundamental-to-effective coarse-graining | derive the effective UCC from a fundamental field while respecting $\Lambda\le0$ |
| Forced-cubic universality class | classify systems where the forced cubic changes transition order |
| Two-pole bifurcation normal form | classify how the interior maximum appears and moves with scale and coupling |

These are the points where the mathematical program can be advanced or killed.

## 11. Conclusion

The mathematical content of FRC, stated carefully, is not a finished theory. It is a structured research program: define coherence and the Lambda field; use the internal/external entropy split to avoid a circular closed ledger; place effective Lambda dynamics in known reaction-diffusion families; retain the established structural results; record the negative results at the same volume as the confirmations; and leave the physical claims as conjectures until the open problems are solved or killed.
