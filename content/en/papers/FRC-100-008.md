# FRC 100.008 — Computational Realization of Lambda-Field Coherence Dynamics

> **Status note:** preprint (v2.0, 2026-07-08). No Zenodo DOI confirmed yet; it will be added when the record is published.

## 1. Role in the 100-series

[[FRC-100-007]] introduces the Lambda-field as a scalar completion of coherence dynamics. [[FRC-100-009]] writes local open-system reciprocity as a transport law. This paper is the computational bridge: `100.007 Lambda field -> 100.008 executable Lambda dynamics -> 100.009 local reciprocity`.

The role of 100.008 is not to introduce another ontology. Its job is to say how the Lambda-field can be represented, updated, diagnosed, and connected to oscillator implementations **without collapsing the field into an algebraic restatement of the state**. The v1 paper identified the main danger: if Lambda is defined pointwise as a direct function of a local entropy density, it has no independent relaxation, no memory, and no legitimate field dynamics. The v2 paper keeps that correction and adds a reproducible reference implementation.

## 2. State, Target, and Field Are Separate Objects

Let the computational state be a normalized density $\rho(x,t)\ge0$, $\int_\Omega\rho\,dx=1$. The theory uses four separate objects: the state field $\rho$; the mesoscopic coherence target $C_K[\rho]$; the equilibrium target field $\Lambda_{\rm eq}[\rho]$; and the actual dynamical field $\Lambda$.

The separation matters. If one writes, pointwise,

$$
C(x,t) = \exp[-s(x,t)/k_*], \qquad
\Lambda(x,t) = \Lambda_0 \ln C(x,t),
$$

then $\Lambda$ becomes only $-\Lambda_0 s/k_*$ — a mirror of the state, not a dynamical field. A mirrored variable cannot honestly carry its own diffusion constant or relaxation time. This is the anti-circularity requirement of the whole paper.

Here $k_*$ is a scale-setting reciprocity parameter — a units bridge, calibrated in the pilot from the mean kernel score of the run, so it is scale-normalized rather than treated as a fitted universal constant.

## 3. Kernel Coherence Target

The local target is mesoscopic, not pointwise. With a smoothed score

$$
\bar{s}_K(x,t) = \int_\Omega K_\ell(x-y)\,s_\gamma[\rho](y,t)\,dy,
\qquad
s_\gamma[\rho] = -\rho\ln(\rho+\epsilon) + {\gamma \over 2} |\nabla \rho|^2,
$$

the kernel coherence target and equilibrium Lambda target are

$$
C_K[\rho] = \exp[-\bar{s}_K/k_*],
\qquad
\Lambda_{\mathrm{eq}}[\rho] = \Lambda_0 \ln C_K[\rho].
$$

Following [[FRC-566-030]], the closed identity $dS + k_* d\ln C = 0$ must not be used as the anchor for open dynamics; the operational object is the open transport loop.

## 4. Dynamic Lambda Equation

The actual Lambda field obeys a relaxation-diffusion equation, and the state responds to the field:

$$
\partial_t \Lambda
= D_\Lambda \nabla^2 \Lambda
- \mu_\Lambda(\Lambda-\Lambda_{\mathrm{eq}}[\rho])
+ J_{\mathrm{ext}},
$$

$$
\partial_t \rho
= D_\rho\nabla^2\rho
- \alpha\nabla\cdot(\rho\nabla\Lambda)
+ \Xi_\rho .
$$

This produces the computational loop $\rho \to C_K[\rho] \to \Lambda_{\mathrm{eq}}[\rho] \to \Lambda \to \rho$: reciprocally coupled but not algebraically identical.

## 5. Reference Implementation A: Finite-Difference Lambda Engine

On a periodic 1-D grid, each step computes the local score, smooths it by FFT convolution with a Gaussian kernel, calibrates $k_*$, computes $C_K$ and $\Lambda_{\rm eq}$, updates $\Lambda$ spectrally, updates $\rho$ by diffusion plus Lambda-guided drift, then clips and renormalizes. The pilot uses two Lambda source windows as attractor regions. Measured fixed-seed metrics:

| Diagnostic | Initial | Final | Change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Entropy $S$ | 5.544985 | 5.534553 | -0.010432 |
| Kernel coherence $\langle C_K\rangle$ | 0.367922 | 0.369636 | +0.001714 |
| Lambda-target mismatch $E_\Lambda$ | 1.001261 | 0.156237 | -84.4% |
| Attractor-window mass | 0.295593 | 0.355097 | +0.059504 |
| Concentration $\max(\rho)/\mathrm{mean}(\rho)$ | 1.036124 | 1.461361 | +0.425237 |

The strongest result is not the small entropy/coherence shift. It is the explicit field separation: the target is computed from $\rho$, the actual Lambda field relaxes toward it with lag, and the state then responds to the field gradient. This is the minimal anti-circularity control.

## 6. Oscillator Implementation

Lambda can also be realized by oscillator dynamics on phases $\phi_i$ with Kuramoto base coupling. A spatial vector $\nabla\Lambda$ cannot be added directly to a scalar phase rate; three scalar channels are admissible:

- **Channel A — scalar frequency bias:** add $\eta_\Lambda\Lambda(x_i,t)$ to the phase rate.
- **Channel B — phase-reference coupling (preferred):** with reference $L(x,t)=C_\Lambda e^{i\theta_\Lambda}$,

$$
\dot{\phi}_i =
\omega_i + \sum_j J_{ij}\sin(\phi_j-\phi_i)
+ \eta\, C_\Lambda(x_i,t)\sin(\theta_\Lambda(x_i,t)-\phi_i).
$$

- **Channel C — projected gradient drive:** $\eta\,n_i\cdot\nabla\Lambda(x_i,t)$, only when a physical orientation vector $n_i$ exists.

The phase-reference channel makes Lambda a coherence clock rather than a dimensionally invalid vector injection.

## 7. Reference Implementation B: Phase-Reference Locking

## 8. Relation to 566.030 and 100.009

The original 100.008 pointed to 566.001; that anchor is stale. The correct anchor is the open 566.030 frame: in open systems the combined ledger is not a closed conservation identity, and the residual must be measured through export channels. 100.008 should be read as an **engine, not a thermodynamic proof**. It supplies fields and fluxes that [[FRC-100-009]] can place into a local open-system law

$$
\partial_t s + k_*\partial_t\ln C + \nabla\cdot J_R = \Sigma_R .
$$

The open reciprocity papers decide which candidate transport objects ($\rho$, $\Lambda$, $C_K$, field mismatch, drift flux, convergence time, oscillator order) survive as measured physical channels.

## 9. Kill Conditions

This paper fails in its computational role if:

1. $\Lambda$ is defined as an instantaneous pointwise algebraic function of $\rho$;
2. $\Lambda$ cannot be updated independently of $\Lambda_{\rm eq}$;
3. the finite-difference engine fails positivity or normalization;
4. $E_\Lambda$ does not decrease in the relaxation pilot;
5. attractor-window mass does not increase in the drift pilot;
6. oscillator coupling adds a spatial vector directly to a scalar phase rate;
7. the phase-reference pilot does not increase order or reduce reference error.

The shipped pilots pass these checks, but passing them is not enough to claim physical ontology.
