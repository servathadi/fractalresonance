# FRC 100.009 — FRC 100.009: Local Reciprocity Dynamics

## 1. Introduction

The global entropy--coherence reciprocity relation established in [[FRC-566-001]] is **directional**, not a closed conservation law. The secure content is that total entropy production is non-negative ($dS_{\text{total}} \ge 0$), while the combined ledger $dS_{\text{total}} + k^{*}d\ln C$ is bounded below only when coherence is created and carries no floor when coherence is destroyed slowly (566.001 v2.0; measured in 100.005 v2.1). The closed form
$$
dS + k^{*}d\ln C = 0
$$
is the reversible, locked-limit boundary case only, relating informational entropy $S$ and coherence $C \in (0, 1]$. A key structural gap remains: how does this relation appear in a local, open, distributed dynamical system with local storage, flux, source, sink, and defect structure? The local reciprocity defect $\delta_R$ developed below is precisely the local reciprocity *current* — expected nonzero exactly where the system is open and driven, and vanishing only in the closed, silent equilibrium.

This paper develops a local field-level formulation of the **Adaptive Resonance Framework (ARF)**. We localize the global reciprocity law into a field continuity-style balance equation and treat the activation operator as derived from local reciprocity defect rather than inserted as an arbitrary constant floor.

## 2. Core Mathematical Formulation

Let $x \in \Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^d$ denote position and $t \geq 0$ time.

### 2.1 Storage and Flux
We define the **local reciprocity storage density** as:
$$
\Phi_R(x,t) = s(x,t) + k^* \ln\big(C(x,t) + \epsilon\big)
$$
where $s(x,t)$ is the local entropy-like density, $C(x,t)$ is the local coherence, and $\epsilon > 0$ is a small regularization parameter.

The **reciprocity flux** $J_R(x,t)$ is closed via a local gradient diffusion law:
$$
J_R = -D_R \nabla \Phi_R
$$
with diffusivity $D_R > 0$.

### 2.2 Local Reciprocity Defect
The open-system local **reciprocity defect** $\delta_R(x,t)$ measures the departure from local balance:
$$
\delta_R(x,t) = \partial_t \Phi_R + \nabla \cdot J_R - \Sigma_R(x,t)
$$
where $\Sigma_R(x,t)$ is the external reciprocity source density.

To avoid circular dependency in numerical settings, the time derivative of storage is computed using a lagged backward finite-difference discretization:
$$
\partial_t \Phi_R^n \approx \frac{\Phi_R^n - \Phi_R^{n-1}}{\Delta t}
$$

### 2.3 ARF Gating Throttle
The Adaptive Resonance gating throttle $\mathcal{A}(x,t) \in [0, 1)$ acts as a state-dependent activation or mobility operator:
$$
\mathcal{A}(x,t) = \tanh\big(\kappa R(x,t) C(x,t) |\delta_R(x,t)|\big)
$$
where $\kappa > 0$ represents the defect sensitivity, and $R(x,t) = 1.0$ is the constant receptivity field. Transport and transformations only become active in regions where reciprocity is open, imbalanced, or driven.

## 3. Minimal Numerical Toy Model

We validate this formulation by simulating the coupled base transport equations on a 1D grid ($N=128$, $L=1.0$):
$$
\partial_t \rho = \mathcal{A}(x,t) \left[ D_\rho \nabla^2 \rho - \chi \nabla \cdot \left( \frac{\rho}{1 + \gamma \rho} \nabla C \right) \right] + \Xi_\rho
$$
$$
\partial_t C = \mathcal{A}(x,t) \left[ D_C \nabla^2 C - \lambda_C (C - C_0) + \beta \rho \right] + \Xi_C
$$
where $\gamma$ is a chemotactic saturation parameter that prevents singular finite-time blow-ups.

### 3.1 Four Activation Regimes

The numerical simulations verify the self-starting properties of the reciprocity defect driver:

1. **Regime A — Closed Silent Equilibrium:** Starting from a homogeneous state with no external source, the reciprocity defect remains zero ($\delta_R = 0$). The activation throttle remains completely dead ($\mathcal{A} = 0.0$), and the system remains frozen and silent.
2. **Regime B — Artificial Floor:** Injecting a small spatial wave perturbation and enforcing a $10\%$ artificial activation floor allows standard diffusion to flat-out and dissipate the perturbation in nominal equilibrium. This highlights the artificial, always-on nature of standard non-gated transport equations.
3. **Regime C — Local Reciprocity Defect:** Starting from a highly localized initial density perturbation with no external source, the system exhibits localized transient activation around the defect. Once the gradient-driven transport relaxes the fields back to a homogeneous state, the defect decays back to zero, and the system dynamically freezes itself back into absolute silence.
4. **Regime D — Open-System Forcing:** Applying a weak, localized, and periodic external reciprocity source continuously perturbs local reciprocity balance, generating a persistent reciprocity defect ($\delta_R \neq 0$). The activation throttle remains localized ($\mathcal{A} \to 1$), and the density and coherence fields undergo spontaneous, self-starting, and persistent spatial pattern formation, maintaining stable activated fronts indefinitely.

## 4. Observations & Shannon Entropy

Instead of a local provisional entropy-like density which can go negative, we define a normalized global Shannon entropy to track macrostate evolution:
$$
p_i = \frac{\rho_i}{\sum_j \rho_j}, \quad S_\rho = -\sum_i p_i \ln(p_i + \epsilon)
$$
During open-system pattern formation (Regime D), $S_\rho(t)$ decreases dynamically as stable spatial aggregation self-assembles under open forcing, supplying a rigorous thermodynamic visualization of order emergence.
