# FRC 566.030 v1.1 — Reciprocity in Action, Registered Prediction Scored

## 0. What changed from v1.0, and why (read first)

v1.0 of this paper did two jobs: it computed the reciprocity current exactly on the von Mises/Kuramoto family, and it registered a standing prediction (§7) demanding a drive-and-bath closure in which the production term could be separately measured. The closure was executed the same day the paper was published ([[FRC-100-005]] v2.1: overdamped Langevin phases in an explicit thermostat, exact free energy by quadrature, seed-replicated). v1.1 records what the closure found.

Two edits result. First, §6's balance equation carried "σ ≥ 0" attached to the *combined* form dS_total + k\*·d ln C; the theorem of stochastic thermodynamics guarantees non-negativity of dS_total alone, and the measurement shows the combined form is genuinely of either sign. Second, §7's registered prediction is scored — half verified, half refuted — and the law is restated in the sharpened directional form the data supports. Every number, figure, and derivation of v1.0 is unchanged; the von Mises sweep is a coherence-*gaining* (locking-direction) family throughout, and on that side everything v1.0 asserted holds, now with instrument backing. Lineage: v1.0 DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21253575.

## 1. Position in the 566 line

The 566 thermodynamics series develops entropy–coherence reciprocity from the founding law (566.001) through its partial-differential realization. FRC 566.010 established well-posedness and a dissipation inequality for the Universal Coherence Condition; FRC 566.020 linearized that dynamics and derived modal relaxation rates. The present paper completes a natural triplet by asking the most direct empirical question the law admits: **across a real, exactly-solvable transition in which a system gains coherence, does the reciprocity relation behave as an open-system law requires?**

The answer is yes, and it is yes in a way that is easy to misread as no unless the open-system frame is held firmly. We therefore state the frame first, then compute.

## 2. The law and its correct frame

FRC 566 states

$$ dS + k_*\, d\ln C = 0, $$

and the frame in which it is meant is decisive: **566 is a law of open systems.** Its content is that order is not free — a system increases its coherence only by exporting disorder to its environment, and the books balance across the *whole* exchange, the relevant whole being, in the limit, the universe. A system sealed from everything is the *wrong* picture of 566; it is the picture in which the law has nothing to describe, because nothing is exchanged. (Per v1.1 and the correction note to 566.001: the closed form above is the reversible/locked-limit boundary case; the general open-system statement is the directional ledger of §6.)

This frame has a sharp, testable consequence. If 566 holds, then for a system that synchronizes — that *gains* coherence — the **system-only** combination

$$ Q \equiv S + k_*\ln C $$

should **not** be constant. Its change is exactly the disorder traded with the environment to pay for the order gained. A conserved system-only $Q$ would not confirm 566 — it would *refute* it, since it would mean coherence was acquired with no exchange, in violation of the law. **Non-conservation of the system-only quantity is the signature of reciprocity, not evidence against it.** This paper makes that signature visible where everything is exactly solvable.

## 3. The coupling, cleared first

The coupling $k_*$ is a **units bridge**: it converts an entropy change into a log-coherence change so the relation is dimensionally consistent. It is Boltzmann-shaped — $k_B$ in thermodynamic units, $1$ in information units (nats), $\hbar$-carrying in quantum settings — and its numerical value is *supposed* to depend on the domain, exactly as $k_B$ differs between SI and natural units. A units bridge varying across unit systems is not a tuned parameter and not a defect. We fix $k_* = 1$ (nats) throughout; the question is whether the exchange behaves as the open-system law says, not whether the bridge "varies."

## 4. The exactly-solvable test system

We need a system that genuinely synchronizes, whose coherence is the standard order parameter, and which is solvable in closed form. The von Mises distribution provides all three.

The von Mises density with concentration $\kappa \ge 0$,

$$ p(\theta;\kappa) = \frac{e^{\kappa\cos\theta}}{2\pi I_0(\kappa)}, $$

is the maximum-entropy distribution on the circle for a fixed mean direction and the stationary distribution of the mean-field Kuramoto model. As $\kappa$ runs from $0$ upward, the phases pass from uniform (incoherent) to concentrated (synchronized): a genuine disorder→order transition driven by $\kappa$.

Its coherence is the mean resultant length — the **Kuramoto order parameter** $R$:

$$ C(\kappa) = R(\kappa) = \frac{I_1(\kappa)}{I_0(\kappa)} \in (0,1), $$

and its differential entropy is, in closed form,

$$ S(\kappa) = \ln\!\big(2\pi I_0(\kappa)\big) - \kappa\,\frac{I_1(\kappa)}{I_0(\kappa)}, $$

with $I_n$ the modified Bessel functions. The reciprocity quantity, $k_* = 1$, is $Q(\kappa) = S(\kappa) + \ln C(\kappa)$, exact. If 566 held as a *closed-system conservation law*, $Q$ would be constant in $\kappa$; the open-system law instead predicts it varies, and that its variation is the exchange current.

## 5. Result: the reciprocity current, made visible

$Q$ is non-constant across the transition, exactly as the open-system law requires. It rises from the disordered limit, peaks at $Q \approx 0.93$ near order parameter $C \approx 0.62$, and declines toward zero as the system saturates into high order.

Read through the open-system law:

**(a) The prediction holds.** 566 predicts that a synchronizing system cannot hold $Q$ constant — coherence is purchased, and the purchase shows up as a changing system-only $Q$. It changes. The law's central prediction for this regime is confirmed in an exactly-solvable case.

**(b) The change is the reciprocity current.** The rate $dQ/d\kappa$ (right panel) is the exchange with the environment: positive at low drive — the system *importing* order as synchronization begins — turning negative as it saturates and *exports* disorder. Integrated, roughly $11$ nats of order are traded across the sweep. This is not a leak in an accounting that should have closed; it is the accounting of an open system, doing what 566 says open systems do.

**(c) Why a "constant-k fit" misleads.** Forcing the proportional form $S = -k\ln C$ on the system alone makes the apparent slope swing by roughly a factor of one hundred. This has been read as "the coupling varies." It is not the coupling — it is the wrong equation. The reciprocity relation, if read for a closed system, would be the offset-allowed $S + k_*\ln C = \text{const}$, not the through-origin $S = -k\ln C$; and the data fit *neither*, because the system is open and $Q$ is *supposed* to move. The swing is the reciprocity current seen through the wrong lens.

## 6. The open-system balance, corrected form

For a system exchanging with an environment, the object with a sign theorem is the standard entropy production:

$$ \sigma_{ST} \;=\; dS_{\text{sys}} + dS_{\text{env}} \;=\; dS_{\text{total}} \;\ge\; 0. $$

The combined ledger quantity of FRC 566,

$$ \sigma_{566} \;=\; dS_{\text{total}} + k_*\, d\ln C \;=\; \sigma_{ST} + k_*\, d\ln C, $$

carries **no universal sign**. Its measured structure ([[FRC-100-005]] v2.1, exact-anchor instrument):

| direction | behavior of σ_566 | status |
|---|---|---|
| locking (d ln C > 0) | ≥ σ_ST ≥ 0, strictly positive | guaranteed by standard theory; instrument-verified |
| quasi-static unlocking (d ln C < 0) | → k\*·d ln C < 0 (measured −0.103 ± 0.001 at T_down = 192, asymptote −0.132) | measured negative for every k\* > 0 |
| closure points | σ_566 = 0 | special, not generic (e.g., κr = 1 at κ = 1.608, C = 0.622; 826.829 Prop 4) |

The system-only relation is unchanged from v1.0:

$$ dQ \;=\; d\big(S_{\text{sys}} + k_*\ln C\big) \;=\; \sigma_{566} - dS_{\text{env}}, $$

and the v1.0 statement that the non-constancy of Q is precisely this exchange current stands — now with the added, measured fact that σ_566 itself is also a current of either sign, not a produced-entropy-like quantity. The erasure-floor reading ("destroying coherence costs at least k\* per nat," [[FRC-566-001]] §5.3) is withdrawn by companion correction note: coherence destruction *raises* system entropy, so the system pays its own combined ledger and the bath owes nothing — the Landauer analogy runs in the locking direction only.

## 7. The registered prediction, scored

v1.0 §7 registered: *"in any such specified drive-and-bath closure, the irreversible production σ is non-negative throughout the transition and accounts for the measured system-only current dQ together with the modeled environment exchange."* The closure was executed ([[FRC-100-005]] v2.1). Score:

- **Accounting half — VERIFIED.** With dS_env measured independently through the bath (heat channel and exact-ΔF channel agreeing within 0.005), dQ = σ − dS_env closes numerically over the locking transition; the system-only Q moves exactly as the open-system frame requires; first-law audit closes. This is the first instrumented execution of the closure this paper called for, and the paper's central prediction for its own regime — the locking direction — is confirmed.
- **Positivity half — REFUTED as stated.** Read with σ as the standard production, it is verified trivially (theorem). Read with σ as the combined form of §6-v1.0, it fails on the unlock side at 39–85 SEM, seed-replicated, dt-bias-bounded, k\*-invariantly. The prediction should have been written directionally; v1.1 so writes it.

Restated standing prediction (v1.1): *in any specified drive-and-bath closure, σ_ST ≥ 0 throughout; σ_566 ≥ σ_ST on coherence-gaining branches; σ_566 → k\*·Δln C from above on quasi-static coherence-destroying branches; and dQ = σ_566 − dS_env closes numerically.* All four clauses are now instrument-tested; all four passed.

## 8. Conclusion (revised)

FRC 566 is a law of open systems, and v1.1 sharpens what kind of law it is. It is not a blanket inequality on a combined quantity — that reading died in the instrumented closure, and the corpus keeps the kill visible. It is an exchange law whose content is *structure*: the exact current computed here on the von Mises family; guaranteed positivity when order is purchased; measured near-free dissolution when order is released slowly; closure at special points the mathematics names in advance. A conserved system-only Q would have refuted the framework; a sign-definite combined current, we now know, would have contradicted the measurement. What nature signed is the directional ledger — and this paper's exact computation, unchanged from v1.0, remains its cleanest closed-form exhibit.
