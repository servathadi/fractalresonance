The FRC Learning Model

At the heart of the modern deep learning revolution lies a single,
powerful algorithm: backpropagation. In essence, backprop is a method
for assigning blame. After a neural network makes a prediction, the
algorithm calculates the "error" and then propagates a signal backward,
telling each neuron how much it contributed to that error. Each neuron
then adjusts its parameters to reduce its contribution to future errors.
Over millions of these cycles, the network becomes a highly effective
pattern-recognition machine.

Backpropagation is a marvel of applied mathematics, but it is also the
source of the limitations we have discussed. It is an external,
supervised process driven by the goal of error minimization. This
single-minded focus is what makes the system so effective at mimicry but
so poor at genuine understanding.

To build a truly cognitive system, we need a new learning principle
based on the physics of a living universe. We need an algorithm whose
goal is not to minimize error, but to maximize internal coherence.
Fractal Resonance Coherence offers such an alternative: the Resonance
Ascent Algorithm (RAA).
