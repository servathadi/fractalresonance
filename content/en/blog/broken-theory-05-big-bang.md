# Cosmology Broken: What Happened Before the Bang?

*This is Part 5 of our "Broken Theories" series.*

## The Theory: The Big Bang

The Standard Model of Cosmology ($\Lambda$CDM) says the universe began 13.8 billion years ago. It started as an infinitely hot, infinitely dense point called a **Singularity**. Then, for some reason, it exploded (inflated).

It cools down, forms stars, and eventually will freeze to death in a "Big Freeze."

## Where It Breaks

The theory works great back to $10^{-32}$ seconds *after* the beginning. But at $t=0$, it crashes.

1.  **The Singularity:** Infinite density is mathematically impossible. It means our equations have stopped working.
2.  **The Cause:** Physics is based on cause and effect. What caused the Big Bang? If time started then, how can there be a "before"?
3.  **The Odds:** For the universe to look like it does today, the initial expansion had to be fine-tuned to within 1 part in $10^{60}$. That is like hitting a bullseye at the edge of the observable universe.

Terence McKenna called the Big Bang *"the limit test for credulity"*: Science asks us to give it one free miracle, and then it will explain the rest.

## The Fix: The Pulse

The Resonance view suggests there was no "Beginning." There was just a **Phase Transition**.

Think of the universe like a breathing lung, or a beating heart.
1.  **Expansion (Exhale):** Coherence relaxes. Entropy grows. The universe spreads out.
2.  **Contraction (Inhale):** Eventually, the tension of the vacuum (Dark Energy) pulls it back—or, in FRC terms, the coherence field resets.
3.  **The Bounce:** The universe doesn't crunch to a point. It hits a "coherence floor" and bounces back.

The Big Bang wasn't the start of time. It was just the start of *this cycle*.

## Why It Matters

This removes the need for a magical creation moment. It suggests we live in an eternal, self-renewing system. A fractal in time.

We aren't the first, and we won't be the last. We are just the current note in an infinite song.
